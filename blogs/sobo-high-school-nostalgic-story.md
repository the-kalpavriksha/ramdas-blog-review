## Introduction

This short nostalgic story was inspired by a **creative writing prompt** in a writing group.  
It reflects on memories from a **SoBo (South Bombay) high school**, capturing moments that many people remember from their school years.

Sometimes the smallest memories — walking past busy streets, watching pigeons gather near signals, or chatting with friends after school — stay with us for a lifetime.

---

## Memories of School Days

School life often feels ordinary while we are living it, but later it becomes one of the most cherished phases of life.

Some memories that often stand out include:

- Walking through the bustling streets of SoBo on the way to school  
- Watching the city wake up during early mornings  
- Friendships that were formed during lunch breaks and classrooms  
- Small adventures after school hours in the city  

These experiences shape our personalities and remain deeply embedded in our memories.

---

## The Spirit of SoBo

South Bombay has a unique cultural character.  
From historic buildings to crowded intersections and lively street scenes, the environment itself becomes a part of one's childhood story.

Moments like watching pigeons scatter as vehicles move, or observing the rhythm of city life, become small but meaningful fragments of memory.

---

## Reflection

Looking back at school days reminds us how powerful nostalgia can be.

Memories from that time often bring:

- A sense of warmth  
- A connection to our roots  
- Appreciation for friendships and experiences  

Even simple scenes from the city can transport us back to those formative years.
