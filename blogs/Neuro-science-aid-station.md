## Introduction

The Hardrock 100 is one of the most challenging ultramarathon races in the world.  
Runners attempt to complete a 100-mile mountain course in extreme terrain and weather conditions.

This blog discusses the idea of a **Neuro Science Aid Station**, inspired by supporting a runner during the race.

---

## The Hardrock Challenge

The race pushes athletes to their physical and mental limits.  
Fatigue, sleep deprivation, altitude, and terrain make it an extraordinary endurance test.

Such extreme environments reveal how the **human brain and body respond to stress and exhaustion**.

---

## Neuro Science Aid Station Concept

The idea of a Neuro Science Aid Station is to observe and support runners using principles from neuroscience.

Possible focus areas include:

- Monitoring mental clarity
- Observing decision making under fatigue
- Understanding the impact of sleep deprivation
- Supporting cognitive resilience during endurance events

---

## Insights

Extreme endurance sports can provide unique insights into human neuroscience.

By studying runners in such environments, we can better understand:

- Brain endurance
- Mental resilience
- Cognitive limits during physical stress
