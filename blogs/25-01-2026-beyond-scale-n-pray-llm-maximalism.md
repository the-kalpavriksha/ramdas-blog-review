# Beyond #ScaleNPray: Why I Don’t Buy LLM Maximalism

**Author:** Ram Ramdas  
**Date:** 2026-03-09  
**Source:** LinkedIn Post  

---

## Overview

In this LinkedIn post, Ram Ramdas critiques what he calls **LLM maximalism** — the belief that simply scaling models, data, and compute will inevitably lead to artificial general intelligence.

The author refers to this mindset as **“Scale & Pray.”**

---

## Core Argument

Large language models are powerful systems, but they have fundamental limitations.

They operate as **next-token predictors**, meaning they generate text based on statistical patterns rather than maintaining an explicit grounded understanding of reality.

Because of this, they can produce **hallucinations or incorrect reasoning**.

---

## Role of LLMs in Future Systems

The author does not dismiss LLMs entirely.

Instead, he emphasizes that they are extremely useful for:

- coding assistance  
- translation  
- conversational interfaces  
- pattern recognition  
- summarisation  

However, he argues that they should **not be treated as the central intelligence layer of complex systems**.

---

## Hybrid AI Architecture

The author aligns more closely with perspectives associated with researchers such as **Demis Hassabis and Gary Marcus**, which emphasize hybrid AI approaches.

Future AI systems may combine:

- neural networks
- symbolic reasoning
- simulators
- reinforcement learning
- explicit world models

In this architecture, LLMs function more like **interfaces or components within a broader system**.

---

## Concerns About LLM Maximalism

The author raises several concerns about exaggerated claims around LLMs:

- overly optimistic timelines for superintelligence
- vague claims about AI surpassing human capability
- belief that scaling compute alone guarantees progress
- ignoring data, energy, and design constraints

He also notes the contradiction where some narratives claim **“no moat in AI”** while large companies build enormous advantages in compute, capital, and distribution.

---

## Perspective from Financial Technology

From the perspective of someone building financial technology platforms, the author argues that LLMs should exist **around decision systems, not inside them**.

Critical systems dealing with:

- risk
- regulation
- capital allocation
- fairness

require **explicit world models and transparent decision layers**.

## Conclusion

The author concludes that LLMs will remain important tools but should be embedded within **grounded, governable hybrid systems**.

Rather than relying on **Scale & Pray**, the future of AI should focus on **world-model realism and hybrid architectures**.
