Author: Ram Ramdas  
Source: LinkedIn Post  

---

## Overview

In this LinkedIn post, Ram Ramdas reflects on a discussion about AI and large language models after watching a conversation between **Steve Eisman and Gary Marcus**.

The post revisits an earlier article where the author criticized what he calls **“Scale & Pray” LLM maximalism**.

---

## Key Point

The author highlights an important clarification raised by **Gary Marcus** in the comments of the earlier article.

The hybrid / world-model approach that the author attributed to **Demis Hassabis** is something Gary Marcus has also been advocating for many years.

---

## Core Idea

The debate centers around two possible directions for AI:

### Cathedrals of Compute
AI progress driven primarily by:

- larger models  
- more compute  
- more data  

This is described as **“Scale & Pray”** LLM maximalism.

### Compass of Cognition
A hybrid approach combining:

- neural networks  
- world models  
- structured reasoning  

This represents a more practical AI architecture for builders of modern technology platforms.
