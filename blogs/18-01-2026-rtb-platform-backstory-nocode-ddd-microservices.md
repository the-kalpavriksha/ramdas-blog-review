# The Backstory of the RTB Platform – NoCode, DDD & Microservices

**Author:** Ram Ramdas  
**Source:** LinkedIn Post  

---

## Overview

In this post, Ram Ramdas shares the backstory behind the **RTB (Real Time Business Services) platform** developed at WonderLend Hubs.

The initiative started as **Mission RTB – “Raise The Bar”** in March 2020 with the goal of dramatically improving the business outcomes delivered to clients through modern financial technology platforms.

---

## The Core Question

The journey began with an important question:

Why must complex financial technology platforms be designed as rigid systems with artificial boundaries and brittleness?

The team realized that the fintech domain is constantly evolving, and systems that resist change will inevitably struggle.

Instead of fighting change, the RTB philosophy encourages **embracing change as a core design principle**.

---

## Inspiration Behind the Platform

The evolution of the RTB platform was inspired by artistic metaphors such as:

- Beethoven's *Emperor Symphony* conducted by **Zubin Mehta**
- The **Shiv Tandav ballet** with music by **Shankar Mahadevan**

These performances illustrate how multiple components can work together in harmony to create something powerful and dynamic.

This metaphor influenced the design philosophy of the RTB platform.

---

## Technology Foundations

The RTB platform is built using modern architecture concepts including:

- **NoCode constructs**
- **Domain Driven Design (DDD)**
- **Microservices architecture**
- **Cloud-native deployment**

These technologies allow financial platforms to evolve continuously and adapt to changing business needs.

---

## Open Sourcing the “Why”

Ram Ramdas emphasizes that the technology community often shares the **“How”** of systems through open-source tools and frameworks.

However, the deeper inspiration — the **“Why” behind the architecture** — is rarely shared.

This post attempts to **open source the thinking and philosophy behind the RTB platform**.

---

## Internal Platform Milestone

The deck referenced in the post was used internally when the team launched the **internal MVP of the RTB platform in July 2022**.

It explains the architectural thinking and inspiration behind the platform’s evolution.

---

## Conclusion

The RTB platform represents an effort to rethink how complex fintech systems are designed.

By embracing change and drawing inspiration from orchestration and performance metaphors, the platform aims to deliver more adaptable and resilient financial technology systems.
