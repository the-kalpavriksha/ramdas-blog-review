# Cathedrals of Compute vs Compasses of Cognition

**Author:** Ramdas  
**Source:** LinkedIn Article  

---

## Introduction

This article discusses two contrasting approaches to building artificial intelligence systems.

The author introduces the metaphor of **“Cathedrals of Compute”** versus **“Compasses of Cognition”** to describe different philosophies in AI development. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## The Cathedrals of Compute

The "Cathedrals of Compute" represent a belief that intelligence will emerge primarily through **massive computational scale and large language models**.

This approach is sometimes described as **“Scale & Pray”**, where increasing parameters, data, and compute power is expected to eventually produce general intelligence. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Key characteristics of this approach include:

- Focus on scaling models
- Reliance on large datasets
- Emphasis on parameter counts and compute resources
- Belief that intelligence emerges from scale alone

However, the author argues that this approach may lead to issues such as hallucinations, brittle behavior, and excessive dependence on opaque systems. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## Alternative Thinking in AI

The article references perspectives from AI researchers such as **Gary Marcus** and **Demis Hassabis**, who both emphasize the importance of structured reasoning and world models.

Despite differences in their views, both researchers highlight that AI systems need more than just scale; they require mechanisms for reasoning, planning, and understanding the world. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## The Compass of Cognition

The author proposes an alternative framework called the **Compass of Cognition**.

Instead of relying solely on scale, this approach emphasizes orientation in a complex space of reasoning and knowledge.

Important components include:

- Explicit world models
- Explainable decision layers
- Hybrid AI architectures
- Real-world grounding
- Governance and accountability

These systems combine neural networks with structured reasoning systems and domain knowledge. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## Scale & Pray vs Compass of Cognition

The article contrasts the two approaches:

### Scale & Pray (Cathedrals of Compute)

- Intelligence expected to emerge from larger models
- Focus on compute, tokens, and model size
- Risk of opaque and centralized systems

### Compass of Cognition

- Intelligence requires structured reasoning
- Hybrid architectures combining multiple components
- Emphasis on explainability and governance

---

## Perspective from Financial Technology

As a builder of financial technology platforms, the author argues that critical systems such as **risk evaluation, regulation, and capital allocation** should not depend entirely on opaque AI models.

Instead, decision engines should rely on **explicit models and auditable decision layers**, with AI assisting rather than replacing structured reasoning systems. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

---

## Conclusion

The article concludes that scaling AI models alone may not lead to genuine intelligence.

Future progress will likely require **hybrid systems that combine machine learning with structured reasoning, world models, and governance frameworks**.

Investing in these "Compasses of Cognition" may lead to more robust and accountable AI systems.
